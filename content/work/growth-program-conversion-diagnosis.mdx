## Situation / Task

At Eoding, the channeling program needed a clearer business story. The program was driving exposure, but exposure alone was not enough for a customer-facing growth narrative. A recruiter or hiring manager should see this case as Growth PM work: I separated the visible traffic lift from the harder question of whether that attention was becoming purchase intent.

The task was to diagnose the funnel honestly, protect the credibility of the sales message, and turn the evidence into the next operating agenda for conversion improvement.

## Action

I first separated 10 participating companies from a comparison group of 5 non-use companies. Instead of matching products by names that could change over time, I defined a stable product-level matching rule and organized 26 analyzable products.

I then analyzed weekly data files and validation assets from the program period. The analysis separated store-wide metrics from target product metrics and tracked views, payment quantity, payment amount, and purchase-flow signals so the story would not collapse into a single vanity metric.

The key operating choice was to treat the program as a funnel, not as a traffic campaign. I converted the internal analysis into 7 anonymized visualization CSVs so sales and account stakeholders could reuse the findings in KPI cards without exposing customer-level data.

## Result

Average weekly product views increased from 10.8 to 849.0, a 78.6x increase, and in-program product views reached 82,356. During the same period, channeling-driven purchases produced 70 payments and KRW 6,677,950 in payment volume.

The result changed the operating interpretation. The program was strong at creating attention, but the next growth constraint was the post-view conversion path. Based on the evidence, I reframed the program as a conversion-improvement model covering product selection, detail page quality, pricing and options, and consultation or response flow.

## KPI Rationale

For a public portfolio card, the clearest lead KPI is the 78.6x increase in weekly product views because it shows the scale of the channeling effect immediately. I keep KRW 6.68M payment volume as a supporting commercial signal, not as a standalone revenue claim. The stronger PM story is the decision to use both metrics together to move the next growth work from exposure acquisition to conversion quality.

## Tools & Tech

- Weekly CSV analysis across the program period
- Validation CSV and JSON review
- Product-level matching rule
- Store-level and product KPI split
- Anonymized visualization CSVs for KPI cards and sales material

## Collaboration

The work connected operations, sales messaging, and customer-facing growth discussions. My role was to turn raw operating data into a clear, defensible narrative that sales and account stakeholders could use without exposing sensitive customer data.

## Lessons

Growth analysis needs to separate volume from quality. A channel can create reach while still revealing a conversion bottleneck. The PM value comes from translating that diagnosis into a next operating system that customers, sales teams, and product teams can act on.
