## Situation / Task

In an airport robot delivery service, the available customer pool was structurally limited by passport-authenticated passengers and airport staff. The service had novelty value, but many potential users did not know the robot existed or did not understand how to place an order. Orders were below target even though the gate environment created long waiting periods where passengers were open to small experience-based interactions.

The task was to increase order conversion without additional marketing budget, new staff, or heavy product changes. This made the problem an operating leverage question: how could existing robot capacity create more demand?

## Action

I reviewed customer behavior patterns such as language, nationality, companion type, and on-site reactions. Family passengers with children were more likely to notice and treat the robot as an experience, so I defined them as a practical field target.

During idle time, I used the control dashboard to run one or two simulation trips per day around boarding gates. Instead of leaving the robot at the station, the operation converted idle status into visible movement and bench-side stopping. I also applied seasonal visual branding, such as summer and winter accessories, so the robot was easier to notice in a crowded gate area.

The operating direction was shared with field staff and related teams so the same exposure pattern could be repeated. I tracked order changes and customer reactions in a monthly report for seven airport merchants, including why stop space and order handling cooperation mattered to the full service flow.

## Result

Average daily orders increased by 7%. The change used existing robot movement, dashboard operation, and field observation rather than paid marketing. The work also gave merchants a clearer view of how visibility, waiting-time behavior, and operational cooperation affected sales.

## Tools & Tech

- Robot control dashboard for simulation routing
- Daily and weekly order monitoring
- Customer behavior and field reaction review
- Merchant-facing monthly reporting
- Repeatable field operation playbook

## Collaboration

The work connected field operators, airport merchants, and internal stakeholders. I translated customer behavior into a repeatable operating pattern, then used reporting to explain why visibility and merchant cooperation mattered.

## Lessons

Growth work in field operations does not always start with a campaign. Sometimes the highest-leverage move is converting unused operating capacity into customer exposure, then measuring whether that exposure changes behavior.
