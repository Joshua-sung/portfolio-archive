## Situation / Task

At Eoding, I worked as an operations manager but framed this project through a Growth PM lens. The company needed to explain the value of an external channel growth program to customers, but a traffic-only message was not enough. If we emphasized view growth alone, we could overstate the impact; if we ignored post-view conversion, we would miss the next operational actions customers actually needed.

The task was to separate exposure growth from purchase conversion quality and turn the analysis into a usable growth strategy.

## Action

I first separated 10 participating companies from a comparison group of 5 non-use companies. Instead of matching products by product names, which can change over time, I defined a stable product-level matching rule and organized 26 analyzable products.

I then analyzed weekly data files and validation assets from the program period. The analysis separated store-wide metrics from target product metrics and tracked views, payment quantity, payment amount, and purchase-flow signals.

The key operating choice was to treat the program as a funnel, not as a traffic campaign. I converted the internal analysis into 7 anonymized visualization CSVs so the findings could be reused in sales deck KPI cards and public-safe case data.

## Result

Average weekly product views increased from 10.8 to 849.0, a 78.6x increase, and in-program product views reached 82,356. During the same period, channeling-driven purchases produced 70 payments and KRW 6,677,950 in payment volume.

The result changed the interpretation of the program. The main issue was not only insufficient exposure; the conversion step after view also needed improvement. Based on this evidence, I reframed the growth program as a conversion-improvement model covering product selection, detail page quality, pricing and options, and consultation or response flow.

## KPI Rationale

For a public portfolio card, the clearest lead KPI is the 78.6x increase in weekly product views because it shows the scale of the channeling effect immediately. I keep KRW 6.68M payment volume as the supporting commercial signal and use the remaining purchase-flow evidence to explain why the next growth strategy should focus on conversion quality rather than exposure alone.

## Tools & Tech

- Weekly CSV analysis across the program period
- Validation CSV and JSON review
- Product-level matching rule
- Store-level and product KPI split
- Anonymized visualization CSVs for KPI cards and sales material

## Collaboration

The work connected operations, sales messaging, and customer-facing growth discussions. My role was to turn raw operating data into a clear narrative that sales and account stakeholders could use without exposing sensitive customer data.

## Lessons

Growth analysis needs to separate volume from quality. A channel can create reach while still revealing a conversion bottleneck, and the PM value comes from translating that diagnosis into the next operating system customers can act on.
