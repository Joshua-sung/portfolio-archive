## Situation / Task

An outdoor AI data collection project required minimum quantities across four weather conditions: clear, rain, snow, and fog. Field shooting dates had to be coordinated with military units at least a month in advance, and only a limited number of dates were available each month.

When I joined mid-project, clear-weather data was accumulating, but rain, snow, and fog data remained far below target. The task was to reduce delivery risk by proving whether the original requirement was physically achievable, rather than treating the gap as a simple execution failure.

## Action

The first issue was that the supporting evidence did not exist in a usable form. Shooting logs were not consistently organized, so I interviewed stakeholders, collected dates before and after my assignment, and cross-checked the list with multiple people.

I then matched each confirmed shooting date against historical weather records and related news sources. The result was rebuilt into a simple evidence table: shooting date, actual weather condition, available field window, and resulting condition ratio.

The reconstructed distribution showed that the large majority of real shooting days were clear, while rain, snow, and fog were structurally limited. I summarized why additional waiting alone would not close the gap and used the evidence to request adjustment of the equal-quantity weather requirement.

## Result

The requirement adjustment was accepted. The project moved forward under a more realistic standard, reducing the risk of missing delivery criteria because of uncontrollable weather conditions.

## Tools & Tech

- Manual field log reconstruction
- Historical weather record matching

## Collaboration

The work connected field operators, project stakeholders, and the client-side requirement owner. I turned scattered memories and missing logs into a defensible evidence packet that supported a practical decision.

## Lessons

When a requirement depends on an uncontrollable external condition, the PM job is to separate execution failure from physical constraint. A weak log system can be repaired if the evidence path is rebuilt carefully enough.
